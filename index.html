How to make a camel origami?

Interesting facts about Camels

  • Camels have wide feet for walking in sand.
  • Camels have long eyelashes and thin, slit nostrils to protect them from blowing sand.
  • Camels are adapted to survive a long time without water and food.



How to make a chameleon origami?

Interesting facts about Chameleon

  1. Their hooded heads help them collect water in the form of dew and to also impress mates.
  2. Swiveling eyes help them pinpoint fast-moving prey.
  3. Color-changing skin helps them blend in, stand out to potential mates and intimidate rivals.



How to make a pigeon origami?

Interesting facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeon's body is boat shaped and streamlined to provide least resistant to air current.
  • The eyes have well developed nictating membrane for protection from air,dust etc.
  • The forelimbs are modified into wings.



How to make a panda origami?

Interesting facts about Panda

  1. Their thick, black-and-white fur coats keep them warm.
  2. To crush tough bamboo, they have strong jaws and large, flat molar teeth.
  3. To pluck and hold bamboo, they have elongated wrist bones that work much like thumbs.



How to make a cicada origami?

Interesting facts about Cicada

  • Their groove provides shelter and exposes the tree fluids, which the young cicadas feed on.
  • Cicadas are active underground, tunneling, and feeding, and not sleeping or hibernating as commonly thought.
  • After the long 2 to 17 years, cicadas emerge from the ground as nymphs.
